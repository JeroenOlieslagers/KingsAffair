About

The clocks chime 23:59:60…
There is a mediator between the past, present and future: King’s College, Cambridge, for one cataclysmic night. Brave the heady futurism of Bunker, roam the prehistoric world and feel infinity amidst the beauty of the Chapel as the sun sets on a paradisiacal place where all rules run free. Leave your body and surrender to the embrace of time, to entertainment that spans all moods, from spiritual bliss to sweaty techno intensity. Dive into history and take a leap forward with the King’s Affair, where there are no gowns to tie you down.

King's Members

Tickets will go on sale to King’s members at 12am on Thursday, 28th of February. Tickets for King’s members cost £72. Don’t stress - you have a week to secure your ticket, with ticket sales for King’s members ending at 06:00am on Wednesday, 6th March. You will need to log in via Raven in order to buy your ticket. Please note you cannot change the name on your own ticket to a non-King's member.

General Release

Tickets will go on sale to members of the University of Cambridge via general release at 12pm on Wednesday, 6th March. General release tickets will cost £87. You will need to log in via Raven to secure your ticket. You can change the name on your ticket until the 7th of June. No name changes are to be made under any circumstances beyond this deadline. You have four hours to apply for a ticket and will be notified if you were successful or not.

Buy Ticket

Alumni and Fellows

Tickets for alumni and fellows will be sold privately via email and cost £107. Please email members@kings.cam.ac.uk in order to secure your ticket. If you are an alumnus/alumna you must include your graduation year in your email. Once we have verified your status, we will provide you with a ticket. Please note that there is a limited amount of tickets available for alumni and fellows and that tickets will be sold on a first come first served basis.

Charity

This year, the King’s Affair is proud to be supporting Cambridge Cyrenians. Cambridge Cyrenians are a Cambridge-based organisation that offer support for homeless people across the city. They provide accommodation services, mental health services and specialist care for older individuals, aiming to create an atmosphere where residents have the support to take control of their lives.

In order to contribute to the amazing work that the Cyrenians do, we would like to ask you to consider giving £3 to support the organisation. This option will be available to you at the checkout - if you’re not comfortable with it, you can simply opt out by unticking the box.
